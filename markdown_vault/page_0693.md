5. Time no bar where circumstances not known.-For laches to operate as a bar, it must be shown that the cestui que trust knew the trustee was the purchaser ; for while the cestui que trust continues ignorant of that fact, he cannot be blamed for not having quarrelled with the sale (m).
6. Distress of cestui que trust.- The effect of the length of time may also be materially influenced by the continued distress of the cestui que trust (n), but *poverty is merely an ingredient in the case, and will not alone displace the bar (a) .
7. Confirmation of the sale.- Of course the cestui que trust may ratify the sale to the trustee by an express and actual confirmation (b) ; and if the cestui que trust choose to confirm it, he cannot afterwards annul his own act on the ground of no adequate consideration (c).
Buta. Requisites of good confirmation.-The confirming party must be sui juris- not labouring under any disability, as infancy or coverture (d) .
But, in the case of real estate a feme covert can, if it be not settled to her separate use without anticipation, confirm the purchase under the operation of the Fines and Recoveries Act(e).
And in confirmation, as in acquiescence, a feme covert who has property, whether real or personal, settled to her separate use, [or belonging to her as her separate property under the recent Act (f),] may confirm the sale.
