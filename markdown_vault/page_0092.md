To give property to one for the support of another is sufficient
to show that a trust was intended. Loring v. Loring, 100 Mass. 340; Andrews
v. Cape Ann Bank, 3 Allen, 313 ; Whiting v. Whiting, 4 Gray, 236.

Facts
must be disclosed showing a fiduciary relation between the parties, as well as
the terms of the trust. Tatge v. Tatge, 34 Minn. 272. The owner of land, having mortgaged it, conveyed to the mortgagee in consideration of a promise to
pay him any surplus received, at the end of three years, and a trust was
created when the mortgagee sold at an increase.
Freer v. Lake, 115 111. 662.
