The wording of this clause has given rise to some difficulty, but it has been interpreted by Pearson, J. by treating
the first part of the clause as relating to concurrent powers
in the tenant for life ; in which case, if the powers under
the settlement are less beneficial to him than those under the
Act, he is entitled to exercise the powers under the Act notwithstanding any restriction in the settlement.
